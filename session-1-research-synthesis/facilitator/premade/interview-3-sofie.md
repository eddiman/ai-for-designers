# Interview 3 — Sofie

**Participant:** Sofie, 22
**Occupation:** University student (part-time café job)
**Bus usage:** A few times a week; also walks and cycles; occasional longer trips to visit family
**Recorded:** 2026-05-23, ~30 min, semi-structured (excerpt)
**Interviewer:** facilitator (shown as **I**)

---

**I:** Thanks for coming in. Can you describe how the bus fits into your week?

**Sofie:** It's a bit of a mix. If the weather's nice I'll walk or take my bike, so I'm not a die-hard bus person. But when it's raining, or I'm running late for a lecture, or I'm doing a closing shift at the café and it's dark, then I'm on the bus. So it's kind of my backup, but it's an important backup.

**I:** When you do take it, how do you plan it?

**Sofie:** I just open an app and check the next departure. I don't really plan ahead, it's pretty last-minute usually. The thing is I look at the time it gives me, but in the back of my head I'm always thinking, is this actually real? Because I've been burned.

**I:** Tell me about being burned.

**Sofie:** Okay so this is the big one for me. Last term I had an exam, and I gave myself loads of time, and the app showed a bus coming. I waited. The countdown went down to "due" and then it just sat on "due" forever. The bus never came. It was a ghost. There was no next one for ages, and I ended up half-running, half-crying to the exam and made it with like two minutes to spare. Ever since then I genuinely do not trust the times. I pad everything. I'll catch the bus before the one I actually need, just in case the app is lying again.

**I:** That sounds really stressful. So accuracy is the core thing for you?

**Sofie:** A hundred percent. If I could trust the number on the screen, my whole relationship with the bus would change. I don't need much else. I just need it to be true. The worst part isn't even waiting — it's that I can't tell the difference between "it's a bit late" and "it's never coming." Those feel identical until it's too late.

**I:** Is there anything you wish the service did that it doesn't?

**Sofie:** The one thing — if a bus is cancelled or there's a problem, I wish it would just tell me. Like, push it to my phone. "The bus you usually get isn't running tonight." Because right now I find out by standing there. That's the only alert I'd actually want. Everything else I can figure out myself.

**I:** Some people talk about wanting a full app that saves their routes, learns their habits, sends lots of notifications. Does that appeal?

**Sofie:** Honestly? Not really. I mean it sounds nice in theory but I know myself, I'm never going to sit down and set all that up. Saved routes, favourites, profiles — I'd skip all the setup and just use the search like I do now. And tons of notifications would annoy me, I'd turn them off in a day. The *one* exception is the cancellation thing, because that's the one that actually ruins my day. But that's about it being reliable, not about it being, like, a whole personalised thing.

**I:** That's a useful distinction. Are there situations where your needs are different?

**Sofie:** Yeah, actually — when I go to visit my family, that's a different city and I don't know the routes at all. *Then* I'm completely dependent on the app and I'm stressed because I don't know which stop, which direction, am I even going the right way. So for unfamiliar trips I want way more hand-holding. But for my normal week, in my own city, I know where I'm going. I just need the times to be right.

**I:** Anything else around accessibility or other people you travel with?

**Sofie:** A bit — my grandmother uses the bus and she really struggles. She can't tell if she's at the right stop, the steps are hard for her, and she'd never use an app at all, so for her it's all about clear signs and the driver being patient. That's a whole other world from me on my phone. I don't know how you design for both of us at once, but I notice it.

**I:** If you could change one thing about catching the bus, what would it be?

**Sofie:** Make the times honest. That's it, that's the whole thing for me. If the app or the sign said "this bus is really coming in five minutes" and it was true, I'd stop padding my journeys, I'd stop the low-level anxiety, I'd actually relax. And tell me if it's cancelled. Reliability first. The fancy stuff can wait — or honestly it can just not happen, I don't need it.

**I:** Thanks, that's really clear. Anything to add?

**Sofie:** Just that I think people like me would rather have a simple thing that works than a clever thing that doesn't. I've got enough apps. I don't want a relationship with my bus. I just want to trust it.
