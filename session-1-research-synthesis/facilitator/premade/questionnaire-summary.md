### Generic prompt
<!-- "Summarize this questionnaire." -->

**Summary**
### **Summary of Bus Travel Questionnaire (n=142)**

**Overall takeaway:**  
The bus experience is primarily broken by **lack of reliable, trustworthy real-time information**. Riders don’t want complex features—they want **certainty about whether the bus is coming and when**.

***

## 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Who responded

* Majority are **regular users**: 53% take the bus at least weekly, including 22% daily.
* Only 10% are infrequent riders → results reflect experienced users.

***

## 🧭 How people currently catch the bus

* **Digital dominates, but not overwhelmingly:**
  * 39% use a transit app
  * 45% rely on physical sources (timetables or stop displays)
* 12% simply **show up and wait**, indicating low trust or low engagement with tools.

***

## 😤 Biggest frustrations

Top issues cluster around **uncertainty and unreliability**:

1. **Not knowing if the bus is coming** (31%)
2. **Late/early vs schedule** (26%)
3. **Incorrect real-time info** (17%)

➡️ Combined: **74% of frustrations are about unreliable timing information**

Secondary issues (crowding, routes, tickets) are much less prominent.

***

## ✅ What riders want most

* **#1 need by far: accurate real-time info (47%)**
* Service reliability/frequency is second (21%)
* Interest in apps or features is relatively low (14%)

➡️ Clear signal:  
**Fix accuracy first; features are secondary.**

***

## 📊 Attitudes and perceptions

### Strong demand for simple reliability

* **79% agree**: “I just want to reliably know when my bus will arrive”

### Weak enthusiasm for new apps

* Only **34% positive** toward dedicated app
* **38% negative**, 28% neutral

➡️ People are skeptical of new tools unless they actually work

### Very low trust today

* **55% do not trust real-time info**
* Only **23% trust it**

➡️ Trust is the core failure in the system

***

## 💬 Open responses (qualitative insights)

Themes reinforce the quantitative results:

* **Certainty matters most**
  * “Just tell me the truth about when it's coming”
* **Real-time info is widely distrusted**
* **Waiting without information is painful**, especially in bad weather
* **Disruptions are poorly communicated**
* Some interest in apps—but only **if accurate**

***

## 🔑 Key insights

1. **This is fundamentally a trust problem**  
   Riders don’t believe the information they receive.

2. **Reliability beats features**  
   People prefer simple, accurate timing over advanced apps.

3. **Real-time systems are failing expectations**  
   Incorrect predictions are as damaging as no information.

4. **Uncertainty drives frustration more than inconvenience**  
   Not knowing is worse than waiting.

5. **Apps are not the solution unless accuracy improves**  
   Better UX won’t fix broken data.

***

## 🎯 Bottom line

> Riders don’t need innovation—they need **truthful, reliable arrival information** they can trust.

Everything else (apps, personalization, features) only matters **after that core need is solved**.


### Directed prompt

### **What frustrates riders today**

Frustration is overwhelmingly driven by **uncertainty and unreliable information**:

* **Not knowing if the bus is actually coming (31%)** — the single biggest issue
* **Buses not matching the schedule (26%)** — arriving late or early
* **Incorrect real-time predictions (17%)** — apps and displays can’t be trusted

👉 Together, these show that **most frustration (≈74%) comes from unreliable or missing timing information**, not physical service issues.

Open responses reinforce this:

* People **don’t trust real-time info**
* They dislike **waiting without knowing what’s happening**
* Lack of communication during **delays or cancellations** adds to the problem
* Bad weather amplifies the frustration of uncertainty

***

### **What people most want improved**

The priority is clear and consistent:

* **Accurate, trustworthy real-time arrival information (47%)** — by far the top request
* **More reliable/frequent service (21%)** — secondary but still important
* **Apps or features (14%)** — lower priority and dependent on accuracy

Supporting sentiment:

* **79% say they just want to reliably know arrival time**
* **55% do not trust current real-time information**
* Interest in new apps is **mixed to negative**, unless they actually work

***

### **Bottom line**

People aren’t asking for new features—they want **confidence and truth**.  
The core problem is **broken trust in arrival information**, and the most important improvement is **fixing accuracy and reliability of real-time updates**.
