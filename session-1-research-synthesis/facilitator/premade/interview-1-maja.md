# Interview 1 — Maja

**Participant:** Maja, 29
**Occupation:** Marketing coordinator
**Bus usage:** Daily — commute plus errands, no car
**Recorded:** 2026-05-20, ~35 min, semi-structured (excerpt)
**Interviewer:** facilitator (shown as **I**)

---

**I:** Thanks for making time. To start, can you walk me through a normal day of using the bus?

**Maja:** Yeah, so I don't have a car, the bus is basically everything for me. Mornings I take the 12 from near my flat into the centre, then most days there's a second leg on the 4 over to the office. Evenings it's the reverse, except messier, because I might go to the gym or meet people, so the route I need changes depending on the night.

**I:** How do you decide when to leave in the morning?

**Maja:** Okay, this is the thing. I've got like three things open at once. I check the transit app first, then I don't fully trust it so I glance at the live map if it bothers to load, and honestly half the time I just leave early and hope. I've built this whole routine in my head — I know the 12 is *supposed* to come at :08 and :23, and I also know the :08 is a liar. So I aim for the :23 but leave as if it's the :08. It's exhausting when you say it out loud.

**I:** That does sound like a lot to hold in your head.

**Maja:** It's so much! And the part that gets me is I take the *same* trips over and over. Home to centre, centre to office. The app makes me type it in fresh every single time, or dig through a search. Why doesn't it just know? I've done this exact journey four hundred times. I want it to go, "morning Maja, leave in six minutes for the 12," and I tap it and I'm out the door.

**I:** So something that learns your pattern.

**Maja:** Yes. Honestly what I want is a proper personal app. Saved routes — home, work, gym, my mum's. Favourites. Notifications that actually mean something, like "leave now" or "your usual bus is delayed, here's the next one." Maybe it knows my calendar so if I've got an early meeting it nudges me earlier. I'd pay for that. I've literally searched the app store for it and everything out there is either a glorified timetable or it's clunky and ugly.

**I:** What's wrong with the ones you've tried?

**Maja:** They're generic. They treat me like a tourist who's never seen a bus. Every time I open it, it's a blank search box and a map zoomed out to the whole region. I don't need the whole region, I need *my four routes.* And none of them remember anything between sessions. It's like having an assistant with amnesia.

**I:** You mentioned notifications. What would good notifications look like for you?

**Maja:** Proactive, not me going to check. Push me a "leave now" based on where I am and live traffic. Tell me before I'm already at the stop that the bus is full or cancelled. And let me set the rules — like, only bug me on weekday mornings, don't ping me on a Sunday. Personalisation is the whole game for me. A bus app that doesn't adapt to me is just a worse version of a paper timetable.

**I:** Let's talk about the worst experiences. Tell me about a bad day.

**Maja:** Oh, easy. A few weeks ago, pouring rain, I'm at the stop, the sign says 2 minutes. Two minutes for, I swear, fifteen minutes. No bus. Then it just... vanishes off the sign entirely, like it never existed. I'm soaked, I'm late, and I have no idea if I should keep waiting or run for the train. That feeling of being *stuck guessing* is the worst. If the app had just told me "this one's not coming, here's your next option," I'd have been fine. I don't even mind the bus being late. I mind not knowing.

**I:** That distinction is interesting — late versus not knowing.

**Maja:** Totally different things. Late I can plan around if you tell me. Silence is what kills me. But see, in my head the fix for all of that is still the same app — it should handle the delay, the alternative, the notification, all of it. One thing that just manages my bus life so I don't have to run this whole operation in my head every morning.

**I:** Outside of the app idea, is there anything about the buses themselves you'd change?

**Maja:** I mean, more frequent would be nice, everyone says that. And the 4 gets rammed at 5pm, you can't get on sometimes. But honestly those feel like big infrastructure things that aren't going to change. The information, the app — that feels actually fixable. That's where I'd put the effort.

**I:** If you imagine the ideal version a year from now, what does it look like?

**Maja:** I open my phone, it already knows it's Tuesday and I go to the office, it says "leave in 4 minutes, 12 then 4, you'll be in at 8:52, the 4 is a bit full today." I tap once. I get a nudge if anything changes. I never type a route again. That's it. That's the dream. It feels small but it would genuinely change how I feel about every single morning.

**I:** Anything else you want to add?

**Maja:** Just — make it feel like it's *mine,* you know? I don't want another generic transit tool. I want something that's learned my life and has my back. That's the difference between an app I tolerate and one I'd actually love.
